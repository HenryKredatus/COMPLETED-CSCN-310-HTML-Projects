Visitor Information

The WWII Aircraft Artist Museum is designed with visitors in mind, offering a comfortable and welcoming environment for families, students, and history enthusiasts. Guests can stop by the Welcome Desk to pick up maps, daily schedules, and tips for navigating the museum. Knowledgeable staff and volunteers are available throughout the building to answer questions and help guide you to exhibits or interactive areas.

Several visitor amenities are available during your stay, including a museum café with indoor and outdoor seating, a gift shop featuring aviation-themed books and artwork, and a quiet reading lounge stocked with historical documents and archival materials. Restrooms and water stations are located on every floor for convenience.

Guests may choose between self-guided exploration or structured visits. Guided tours run twice daily and provide insights into the artwork, the artists, and the stories behind the aircraft. For visitors who prefer a flexible approach, mobile-friendly audio tours are available and offer optional detailed commentary about key pieces.

The museum is committed to accessibility for all visitors. Public areas are wheelchair-friendly, elevators allow access to each exhibition level, and service animals are fully welcome. Monthly sensory-friendly visiting hours create a quieter experience for guests who benefit from reduced noise and attendance.

Whether you're planning a full afternoon of exploration or making a short stop, the museum aims to make every visit enjoyable, educational, and stress-free.
